What This Is

When you travel, you often move from place to place.

You follow maps.
You check recommendations.
You try not to miss anything.

But once you’re there, something is missing.

No one really shows you how to experience what you’re seeing.

That’s what Deeply Slow Travel is for.

What We Do

We create guided walking experiences that help you:

  • notice more
  • understand what you’re seeing
  • feel more connected to each place

Not by adding more information,
but by guiding your attention.

How It Works

Each experience is designed to be used as you walk.

You listen.
You look again.
You notice something you would have missed.
You take a moment to reflect.

That’s it.

About

Deeply Slow Travel started from a simple observation:

Even in beautiful places, it’s easy to move through them without really experiencing them.

There is plenty of information —
but very little guidance on how to engage with it.

Our Idea

We believe that small moments, when experienced with attention, can be more meaningful than trying to see everything.

So we design experiences that help you slow down —
not in time, but in awareness.

Created by Alessandra Velho.

The Experience

A Simple Structure

Each walk follows the same idea:

A short story

to help you understand where you are

A detail to notice

something most people overlook

A prompt

to guide your attention

A moment to reflect

your own experience

What Changes

You don’t just pass through a place.

You engage with it.

You start to see more,
and remember more.

You arrive at a square.

At first, it’s just movement — people, sounds, passing moments.

You learn something that isn’t visible anymore.

You are invited to look again.

And suddenly, the place feels different.
